The Aurum Century Club is a Singapore Variable Capital Company deploying member capital into physical gold — specifically LBMA Good Delivery bars, one kilogram each, held in allocated custody in Singapore's Free Trade Zone. Aurum manages the vehicle. Members hold interests in it. The gold does not move unless you do.

Every subscription participates in a credit facility of up to 75% LTV against the gold collateral — a structural feature of the vehicle, not an election. The facility funds private credit deployments selected by the investment committee. Members are not required to draw on it, but the returns it generates flow to all holders.

The return architecture is three-layered: gold appreciation over time, private credit yield from the LTV facility, and the LTV arbitrage itself. The vehicle targets a net IRR of approximately 15% per annum. This figure is illustrative and not a guarantee. A hurdle of 8% per annum, compounding, applies. Carry is 20% after a full 100% catch-up. Management fee is 2% per annum on committed capital.

The Founding Round is limited to one hundred members. The vehicle does not reopen. Once the cohort is full, no further subscriptions are accepted on founding terms.

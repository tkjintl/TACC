## 1. Opening Statement

The Aurum Century Club is a Singapore-registered variable capital company established to hold physical gold on behalf of a founding cohort of 100 members. Each member holds an LP interest in a fund whose principal asset is one or more LBMA-compliant one-kilogram gold bars, held in allocated, segregated custody at Singapore Freeport and collateralised against an institutional credit facility deployed into private credit and pre-IPO equity positions. The fund structure sits under the Singapore Variable Capital Companies Act. It is not a commodity pool, not a digital asset vehicle, and not a yield product. It is a managed fund whose base position is physical gold, with a secondary credit and equity sleeve funded by the credit line drawn against that gold.

The inaugural close is Q3 2026. 100 seats. No reopening after close.

---

## 2. The Gold Position

The fund's primary asset is physical gold. Bars are procured from LBMA-approved refiners, delivered to Malca-Amit Singapore Freeport, and entered into allocated, segregated custody under the fund's sub-account. Each bar carries a refiner serial number, a hallmark, and a weight assay; these details are recorded in the fund's bar-level registry, which is reconciled by the independent fund administrator at subscription and updated upon any change to the inventory.

Allocated custody means the bars are not pooled with any other account. They are physically identified to the fund and insured under Malca-Amit's institutional insurance programme. In a custodian insolvency, the bars sit outside the custodian's balance sheet and are subject to the ring-fencing provisions of the custody agreement.

An independent physical inspection is conducted annually. The resulting vault verification report is made available to all members through the document archive. Members do not take direct delivery of bars during the fund term.

## 3. The LTV Structure

The fund operates a gold-collateralised credit facility with a Singapore institutional bank. The facility is drawn against the fund's allocated gold position. The Fund Agreement imposes a minimum equity covenant of 50% — meaning the loan balance cannot exceed 50% of the gold position's value at LBMA spot — and a maximum LTV ceiling of 75%.

Within that 50–75% band, the Manager draws on the facility to deploy capital into the credit and equity sleeve. The 20% fund-level cash reserve, funded from the remaining 20% of each member's subscription wire, sits outside the credit line and is maintained as a first response to LTV stress: if gold falls materially, the reserve is applied to principal reduction before the bank's margin threshold is approached.

Members have no direct obligation to the bank. The credit facility is an obligation of the fund entity. Members may, however, receive capital calls as provided in the Fund Agreement and PPM §7.4 — these are not a recourse mechanism but a mechanism for pro-rata reserve replenishment and, where applicable, trough-acquisition of additional gold. Capital calls are capped in aggregate at 100% of the member's original commitment over the term.

## 5. Fund Lifecycle

**Stage 1 — Subscription**
The member executes the subscription agreement and NDA, which have been reviewed and approved by the Manager. This stage is complete when the Manager's countersignature is issued.

**Stage 2 — Wire**
The member remits the subscription amount by international wire to the fund administrator's designated account, quoting the member's reference number on the wire memo. Wire receipt triggers the procurement sequence. 80% of the wire proceeds to gold procurement; 20% goes to the fund-level cash reserve.

**Stage 3 — Position Active**
On bar delivery and serial registration, the member's LP interest is established in the fund register. The member receives a confirmation letter stating their allocated kilograms, bar serial numbers, and member number. Portfolio access in the member portal reflects live gold pricing against the cost basis from that date.

**Stage 4 — Capital Calls (if any)**
If the Manager issues a capital call under PPM §7.4, the member receives written notice with the call amount, purpose, and settlement deadline. Capital calls are pro-rata across all members. No member may be called for more than 100% of their original commitment in aggregate over the term.

**Stage 5 — Annual Reporting**
Each year, the Manager distributes a K-1 equivalent, an audited fund financial statement, a position summary with cost-basis support, bar custody certificates, and the vault verification report. These are available in the member document archive within 90 days of the fund's fiscal year-end.

## 6. Member Obligations

**Members must:**

- Execute the subscription agreement and NDA prior to the Manager's countersignature
- Remit the subscription wire in full, with the correct reference, within the deadline specified in the wire instructions
- Maintain a valid email address registered with the fund administrator for all formal notices
- Engage qualified Korean tax and FX counsel for all Korean-side reporting obligations; the fund does not provide Korean tax advice
- Notify the Manager in writing of any change to contact details, beneficial ownership, or material change in circumstances relevant to the subscription representations

**Members must not:**

- Share access credentials, login codes, or document links with any person not named as an authorised user of this member's account
- Attempt to transfer or assign their LP interest without prior written approval from the Manager per PPM §10.4
- Represent to third parties that the fund guarantees returns, capital preservation, or any specific outcome
- Forward fund documents beyond the scope authorised by the NDA

## 7. Access and Reporting

Each member receives the following through the member portal at `www.theaurumcc.com`:

**Quarterly letters** — Published within 45 days of each quarter-end. Cover gold position summary, credit line status, vault status, and a conditions narrative. Not investment advice. Members are notified by email when each letter is published.

**Annual vault verification report** — Published following the independent annual physical inspection at Malca-Amit. Confirms bar serials, weights, and custody status. Members are notified by email on publication.

**Annual member statement** — Includes audited fund financials, K-1 equivalent, cost-basis reconciliation, and LP position summary. Available in the Documents archive.

**Capital call notices** — Issued by email and in the portal when applicable. Include call amount, purpose, payment deadline, and cure period.

**Document archive** — Subscription agreement, NDA, PPM, structural memo, all letters, all vault reports, and all annual statements. Accessible from the Documents tab. Documents are watermarked with the member's name and member number.

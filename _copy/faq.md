**Q1. How is the gold stored, and who verifies it?**

The fund holds LBMA-compliant one-kilogram bars in allocated, segregated custody at Malca-Amit Singapore Freeport. Allocated custody means each bar is physically identified to the fund's sub-account — it is not commingled with any other account and does not appear on the custodian's balance sheet. An independent third-party inspection is conducted once per year; the resulting vault verification report, confirming bar serials, weights, and custody status, is published to the member document archive and is available to all members. Members may not arrange their own inspections during the term; all verification is conducted through the fund's annual programme.

**Q2. What happens if the gold price falls significantly?**

The fund maintains a 20% fund-level cash reserve, funded from each member's subscription wire at the time of onboarding. If gold falls materially and the loan-to-value ratio on the credit facility approaches the 75% ceiling set in the Fund Agreement, the Manager deploys the reserve to reduce loan principal — restoring LTV headroom before the bank's threshold is reached. If the reserve is fully deployed and the drawdown continues, the Manager may issue a pro-rata capital call to replenish it. Capital calls are capped in aggregate at 100% of the member's original commitment over the fund term. Members should size their initial subscription with the possibility of that contingent capital exposure in mind, as disclosed in PPM §7.4.

**Q3. Can I withdraw my capital before the fund closes or before the end of the term?**

No. The fund's LP interests are illiquid during the term. Subscriptions are not redeemable on demand. The base term is five years from first close, with up to two one-year extensions subject to Member consent thresholds in the Fund Agreement. Limited secondary transfers may be considered by the Manager on a case-by-case basis under PPM §10.4, but the Manager has no obligation to approve any such transfer and no secondary market exists for these interests. Members should commit only capital they can hold for the full term, including any extensions — up to seven years.

**Q4. How are Korean resident members handled for tax purposes?**

The fund is a Singapore VCC and does not withhold Korean tax or file Korean tax returns on behalf of members. Korean resident members are required to engage qualified Korean tax and foreign exchange counsel to handle Korean-side reporting obligations, which include initial remittance reporting, annual overseas-account declarations, and Korean tax on distributions. The fund provides standardised annual documentation — including a K-1 equivalent, annual position summary with cost-basis support, bar custody certificates, and audited fund financials — to support each member's Korean counsel in preparing the relevant filings. Aurum does not provide Korean tax or regulatory advice; the adequacy of any filing is the member's responsibility.

**Q5. What is the minimum subscription? Is there a maximum?**

The minimum subscription is one kilogram of LBMA-grade physical gold at first close. At an illustrative LBMA spot price of USD 4,720 per troy ounce, one kilogram equates to approximately USD 151,800 — though the actual USD equivalent will reflect gold spot at the time the fund executes the bar purchase, not the price at IOI submission (see Q9 below). The one-kilogram minimum satisfies the Singapore Securities and Futures Act §305(2) restricted-scheme minimum of SGD 200,000 per subscription. There is no stated maximum, though the Founding 100 is a single-tier cohort — 100 seats, by invitation — and each subscription occupies one seat regardless of commitment size. Higher commitment levels carry proportionally higher LTV access per the Fund Agreement.

**Q6. How does the credit line work, and who bears the risk of it?**

The credit facility is drawn by the fund entity against the gold position held at Malca-Amit. The proceeds are deployed into private credit and pre-IPO equity positions per the Fund Agreement's investment criteria. Members do not sign the credit facility documentation and have no direct obligation to the bank. The risk members bear is through their LP interest: if the credit sleeve produces losses, or if a forced deleveraging event occurs at an unfavourable gold price, NAV is reduced accordingly. The fund does not represent that the credit line will generate a specific return; deployment and yield are subject to market conditions and opportunity availability. Full risk disclosures are in the PPM.

**Q7. What happens when a capital call is issued?**

The Manager will issue written notice to each member specifying the call amount (pro-rata to original commitment), the purpose — typically reserve replenishment following reserve deployment or trough-acquisition of gold at a material drawdown — the payment deadline, and the cure period. Capital calls are mandatory for continuing members. A member who fails to fund a capital call within the cure period is subject to dilution or other remedies as specified in the Fund Agreement. Aggregate capital calls per member are capped at 100% of original commitment over the term. The Manager does not issue capital calls for purposes outside those specified in the Fund Agreement and PPM §7.4.

**Q9. What gold price determines my allocation — the price on the day I subscribe, or the day my wire arrives?**

Your kilogram allocation is determined at the LBMA spot price at the time the fund executes the bar purchase, not at the time of IOI submission or wire dispatch. The fund targets execution promptly following wire clearance. Gold spot may move between IOI submission and trade execution; any movement is borne by the member, with the corresponding effect on kilogram allocation. The fund commits to the wired USD amount, not to a fixed kilogram quantity at subscription. Your actual per-kilogram cost will slightly exceed LBMA spot, reflecting dealer spread, insurance attachment, and bullion execution costs — standard for physical bar procurement.

**Q10. Is this fund regulated? Where is it registered?**

The Aurum Century Club is a Singapore variable capital company (VCC) registered under the Variable Capital Companies Act 2018. The fund is offered on a restricted basis to not more than 50 investors as a private scheme under Singapore Securities and Futures Act §305(2), which exempts it from the requirement to lodge a prospectus with the Monetary Authority of Singapore. This exemption does not mean the fund is unregulated — the VCC structure, the Manager, and the custodian are all subject to applicable Singapore law. Members should engage their own legal counsel to confirm that the fund's regulatory status is appropriate for their jurisdiction of residence, particularly for Korean resident investors who are subject to separate Korean financial regulation.

**Q12. What is a VCC?**

A Variable Capital Company is a Singapore corporate structure designed specifically for investment funds, introduced under the Variable Capital Companies Act 2018. Unlike a standard private limited company, a VCC may redeem and issue shares without requiring a formal capital reduction, and its assets are statutorily segregated from those of its sub-funds under VCC Act §29. The Aurum Century Club is structured as a single-fund VCC — there is one sub-fund, one gold position, and one credit sleeve. The VCC structure provides members with clear statutory asset segregation, clean annual audited accounts at the fund level, and a Singapore-domiciled fund entity that is familiar to international private banks and institutional counterparties.

**Q13. What are the exit terms?**

Exit occurs at the end of the fund term — five years from first close, with up to two one-year extensions subject to Member consent. At wind-down, the gold position is liquidated or distributed in kind (at the Manager's election and subject to the Fund Agreement), credit and equity positions are realised, the credit facility is repaid, and net proceeds are distributed to members pro-rata to their LP interests after fees and expenses. There is no guaranteed return of capital; exit proceeds depend on gold price at the time of liquidation, credit sleeve performance, and the state of equity positions at term. The fund does not offer early redemption. Limited secondary transfers require Manager approval under PPM §10.4; no secondary market exists and no liquidity should be assumed before term-end.
